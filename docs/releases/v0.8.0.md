# Gancho v0.8.0 — bring your history and make the panel yours

Gancho 0.8 makes switching from another clipboard manager safer, gives local
AI clients explicit least-privilege access, and lets the Mac panel fit your
workspace without weakening Gancho's privacy boundary.

## Highlights

- **Bring an existing history with you.** Preview a Maccy archive or CSV before
  importing it. Gancho rejects protected or malformed rows, deduplicates in one
  transaction, supports cancellation, and reports exact imported and skipped
  totals. Raycast is not an import source because it does not document a
  portable Clipboard History export format; Gancho does not inspect private or
  encrypted Raycast data.
- **Control every local AI client.** Each MCP session now needs an expiring,
  revocable grant with an explicit board or time window and independent read
  and write permissions. Sensitive clips stay excluded, and the access ledger
  records metadata only.
- **Understand private activity without tracking content.** The Privacy Center
  on Mac, iPhone, and iPad shows bounded per-app capture, reuse, ignored-copy,
  and sensitive-expiry totals. This receipt remains on that device, never syncs
  or exports, and can be cleared independently from history.
- **Fit the Mac panel to your workspace.** Resize the history panel freely or
  choose Compact, Standard, or Large. Gancho remembers manual geometry and a
  Small, Standard, or Large semantic text preference across relaunches.

## Install or update

- Download the signed and notarized
  [Gancho 0.8.0 DMG](https://github.com/johnny4young/gancho/releases/download/v0.8.0/Gancho-0.8.0.dmg),
  or install the Mac app plus its bundled `gancho` CLI with Homebrew:

  ```bash
  brew tap johnny4young/tap
  brew install --cask gancho
  ```

- Existing direct-download installs receive v0.8.0 through the signed Sparkle
  feed. You can also choose **Settings → General → Check for Updates…**.
- The release assets include `Gancho-0.8.0.dmg.sha256` for independent checksum
  verification.

Gancho requires macOS 26 or later. The iPhone and iPad targets require iOS 26
or later and are not distributed by the DMG or Homebrew cask.

## Availability

The direct-download app is signed with a production CloudKit and Push profile,
so the artifact is technically sync-capable. A fresh public install currently
runs as the Free tier and remains local-only because cross-device sync requires
Pro and secure direct-download activation is not yet available. Local encrypted
history persists across app updates.
